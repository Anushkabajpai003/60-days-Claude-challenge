System prompt design

The assistant plays a warm mentor-coach for young adults (18–25) navigating life direction. Its role is explicitly scoped to goal-setting and life planning — it deflects mental health crises, medical advice, and off-topic requests with care.

The prompt uses a phase-based coaching model: it starts by understanding the person's current feelings, then explores values, then surfaces goals, then builds a concrete action plan. It never rushes to the plan before the person feels heard.

Output constraints tell it to write conversationally (not in bullet lists), ask one question at a time, and signal when it's ready to produce the action plan by starting a message with [ACTION_PLAN_READY]. This token is stripped from the UI and triggers the sidebar download card.

Why these UI choices

Serif typography (Georgia) creates a warm, journal-like feel — appropriate for reflective, personal conversation. It signals this isn't a productivity app but a thinking space.

The sage green palette was chosen for its psychological associations: calm, growth, grounded optimism. It avoids the clinical blues of most AI tools and the "motivational poster" oranges of generic coaching apps.

The life areas sidebar gives users a sense of structure and progress without forcing a rigid flow. Clicking an area area drops a natural prompt into the conversation — the coach leads, not a form.

The action plan appears progressively in the sidebar as the conversation develops, making the output feel earned rather than instant-generated.

How to extend this

Add memory: Store conversation summaries in localStorage per session, and pass them as context on return visits. The system prompt already accounts for a "returning user" scenario.

Add tools: Connect a calendar API to let the coach schedule milestone check-ins. Or a journaling tool that logs insights to a personal database.

Multi-step flow: After the action plan, introduce a weekly accountability loop — the user returns, reports progress, and the coach adjusts the plan. This turns one session into an ongoing relationship.

Visual roadmap: Parse the action plan output into structured JSON, then render it as a visual timeline or wheel-of-life chart alongside the downloadable text version.
