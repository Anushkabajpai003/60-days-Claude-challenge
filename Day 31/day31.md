# Day 31 — AI Supply Chain Control Tower

## Project Overview

Today I built **AI Supply Chain Control Tower**, an interactive operations simulation that places the player in the role of a Head of Operations managing disruptions across a global supply chain network.

The objective is to respond to operational incidents, protect business performance, and maximize key performance indicators before the simulation timer expires.

---

## Project Goals

* Simulate real-world supply chain operations
* Practice decision-making under pressure
* Understand trade-offs between cost, speed, service, and risk
* Visualize operational KPIs in a control tower environment
* Create an engaging learning experience using only HTML, CSS, and JavaScript

---

## Technologies Used

* HTML5
* CSS3
* Vanilla JavaScript

No external libraries, frameworks, APIs, or backend services were used.

## Operational Summary

The operation maintained strong service performance while managing multiple disruptions simultaneously.

Proactive actions such as inventory transfers, alternate routing, backup supplier activation, and expedited shipments helped protect customer service levels and reduce operational risk.

Although several disruptions increased costs temporarily, overall performance remained stable and customer satisfaction stayed high.

The final outcome demonstrated effective prioritization and decision-making under pressure.

---

## Key Learnings

### 1. Every Decision Has Trade-Offs

Faster solutions often increase operating costs, while cheaper solutions can impact customer satisfaction and service levels.

### 2. Visibility Improves Response Time

A centralized control tower allows teams to identify disruptions quickly and take corrective action before issues escalate.

### 3. Risk Management Is Critical

Small disruptions can rapidly become major operational problems if not addressed promptly.

### 4. KPI Monitoring Drives Better Decisions

Tracking service level, inventory health, transportation efficiency, and cost simultaneously leads to more balanced decision-making.

### 5. Supply Chains Are Dynamic Systems

Operational conditions continuously change, requiring constant monitoring and adaptation.
