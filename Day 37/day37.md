# Day 37 – Task Compass

## Project Overview

Task Compass is an interactive workplace simulation designed to teach how work flows through real organizations. Rather than testing knowledge of job titles, it helps users understand ownership, delegation, collaboration, escalation, and workflow management through realistic workplace scenarios.

## Key Features

* Three-stage management simulation
* Drag-and-drop interactions
* Ownership identification challenges
* Task routing and workflow mapping
* Collaboration-based decision making
* Progress tracking throughout the experience
* Understanding-focused feedback system
* Final reflection and insights dashboard
* Dark mode glassmorphism UI
* Responsive offline-first design

## Stages

### Stage 1: Who Owns This?

Users analyze workplace tasks and identify the primary owner responsible for driving the issue toward resolution while learning which supporting roles contribute.

### Stage 2: Task Routing

Users build realistic workflows by arranging roles in the order work typically moves through an organization.

### Stage 3: Collaboration Challenge

Users tackle larger organizational problems by assigning multiple teams and understanding how cross-functional collaboration leads to better outcomes.

## What I Learned

* Ownership does not mean working alone.
* Effective delegation improves organizational efficiency.
* Most workplace problems require collaboration between multiple teams.
* Communication flow is often as important as technical execution.
* Organizations function best when responsibilities are clearly defined.

## Key Insight

Many real workplace problems are solved by coordinated teams rather than individuals. Understanding who owns a task, who supports it, and how information flows between stakeholders is essential for successful execution.
