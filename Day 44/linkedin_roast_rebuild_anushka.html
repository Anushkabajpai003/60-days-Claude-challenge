Headline

Recruiter's 3-second reaction
"Another fresh grad with no LinkedIn. What do they actually do?"
❌ Problem: You have no headline — LinkedIn defaults to "Student at [University]" or blank. That's not a headline, that's a non-presence.
🧠 Why it hurts: Recruiters do keyword searches. "Cloud computing," "Java," "SQL" — none of these appear in your headline, so you don't show up when they search.
🔍 Invisible cost: You're losing the top 3 inches of prime real estate on your profile. Most recruiters never scroll past the headline + photo.
1/10

About — First 2 Lines (The Hook)

Recruiter's 3-second reaction
"Nothing here. They didn't bother."
❌ Problem: You have no About section at all. LinkedIn shows the first 2 lines before "see more" — right now that space is blank.
🧠 Why it hurts: A blank About tells recruiters you're either lazy or haven't taken yourself seriously as a candidate yet. Both are dealbreakers in the first 3 seconds.
🔍 Invisible cost: You're handing this prime hook real estate to silence. Every recruiter who lands on your profile leaves immediately.
3/10

Experience

Recruiter's 3-second reaction
"Projects are listed, but I have no idea what they did or what the impact was."
❌ Problem: You have projects (restaurant website, face recognition attendance system, cloud training) but they likely read as one-liners without context, numbers, or outcomes.
🧠 Why it hurts: "Built a restaurant website" means nothing. "Built a responsive restaurant website reducing booking inquiry time by 40%" is a candidate. Recruiters need proof, not descriptions.
🔍 Invisible cost: Your best work looks like a class assignment. Recruiters can't differentiate you from 100 other fresh grads who "did projects."
4/10

Skills & Keywords

Recruiter's 3-second reaction
"Cloud, Java, SQL are there but not specific enough to surface in ATS filters."
❌ Problem: Generic skill names without specificity. "Cloud Computing" isn't a searchable skill — "AWS," "GCP," "Azure" are. Recruiters filter by exact tool names.
🧠 Why it hurts: ATS systems and LinkedIn search use exact-match keyword filters. "Cloud Computing" won't surface you when a recruiter types "AWS Engineer."
🔍 Invisible cost: You're being filtered out before a single human eye sees your profile.

Headline — 3 options

Option 1 — Keyword-optimized (for search)
Cloud Computing & Java Developer | SQL | AWS | Detail-Oriented Problem Solver | Open to Opportunities
Option 2 — Value proposition (for recruiters)
CS Graduate | I build reliable systems and catch what others miss | Cloud · Java · SQL
Option 3 — Authority style (for thought leadership)
Troubleshooter & Detail-First Developer | Cloud Infrastructure · Java · SQL | Turning overlooked bugs into bulletproof solutions
Use Option 1 when applying to jobs — maximum keyword density.
Use Option 2 once you have 1–2 months of job search going — it feels human and memorable.
Use Option 3 when you start posting content and building a niche reputation.

That instinct — to dig before you guess — is what separates developers who ship from developers who scramble.
