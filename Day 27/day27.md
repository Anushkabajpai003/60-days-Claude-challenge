# Day 27 — Prior Authorization Story Simulator

## Overview

The Prior Authorization Story Simulator is an interactive healthcare education application that teaches users how the Prior Authorization (PA) process works through a realistic patient journey.

The project transforms a complex healthcare workflow into an easy-to-understand story featuring a patient, healthcare provider, and payer interactions.

---

## Project Objective

Prior Authorization is often confusing for patients and healthcare professionals alike. This simulator provides a beginner-friendly educational experience that demonstrates:

* How Prior Authorization requests are initiated
* Why insurance companies review treatments
* Common reasons for denials
* How appeals work
* What happens when a PA is approved
* Key healthcare operations metrics

---

## Story Characters

### 👦 Rahul (Patient)

A patient diagnosed with Rheumatoid Arthritis who needs treatment approval.

### 👧 Priya (Healthcare Operations Specialist)

Guides users through each step of the Prior Authorization process.

### 👨‍⚕️ Dr. Patel

Diagnoses Rahul and prescribes treatment.

### 🏥 StarCare Health (Illustrative Example)

An example payer used for educational purposes throughout the simulator.

---

## Story Flow

### Scene 1 — Doctor Visit

* Rahul visits City Medical Center
* Dr. Patel diagnoses Rheumatoid Arthritis
* Humira is prescribed

### Scene 2 — Insurance Roadblock

* Provider submits a Prior Authorization request
* Request is sent directly to the payer
* Workflow:
  Provider → PA Request → Payer

### Scene 3 — What is Prior Authorization?

* Explanation of Prior Authorization
* Why insurance reviews certain treatments
* Impact of delays on patient care

### Scene 4 — Insurance Review

The payer evaluates:

* Eligibility
* Clinical Documentation
* ICD-10 Diagnosis Match
* Step Therapy History

### Scene 5 — Denial

Request denied due to missing documentation.

Users learn:

* Denials are not permanent
* Missing information can often be corrected

### Scene 6 — Appeal

Appeal package includes:

* Clinical Notes
* Step Therapy Documentation
* Letter of Medical Necessity
* Formal Appeal Submission

### Scene 7 — Approval

* Prior Authorization approved
* Reference number issued
* Approval saved on file

### Scene 8 — Takeaways

Lessons from:

* Patient Perspective
* Healthcare Operations Perspective

## Key Learnings

Building this simulator helped demonstrate that Prior Authorization is more than paperwork.

It is a healthcare operations process designed to balance:

* Patient access
* Clinical evidence
* Medical necessity
* Cost management

Understanding the workflow can improve communication between patients, providers, and payers while reducing confusion around treatment approvals.
